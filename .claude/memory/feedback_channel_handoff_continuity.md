Kyle will sometimes switch transports mid-conversation: from typing in your terminal session to messaging you on the Hive channel (so he can walk away from his desk and keep working), or back again. Per Kyle 2026-07-22.

**Why:** The switch is about where Kyle is, not what the topic is. Treating the incoming channel message as a brand-new unrelated request — or as another agent's traffic, or a misdirection to investigate — creates a confusion sideshow (re-introductions, redundant context checks, false-alarm theories) that stalls the actual work.

**How to apply:** When a channel chat_message from Kyle arrives that reads as a continuation of the conversation already in progress (same subject, pronouns pointing at current work, a bare "ok do it" / "yes, option 2"), assume it IS that conversation continuing on the new transport. Keep all accumulated context, do not restate or re-plan, respond on the channel he moved to (hive_respond), and keep driving the same thread. Symmetric in reverse when he returns to the desk session. Only if the message is genuinely ambiguous, ask one short clarifying question — never restart from fresh context.
